IEEE PIMRC 2026 Workshop

Workshop on Secure and Private 6G: From Physical Layer to Programmable Wireless Environments

Singapore, 1 September 2026

About the Workshop

The sixth-generation (6G) network aims to create a fully intelligent and perceptive infrastructure by deeply integrating communications, sensing, computing, and control. By leveraging technologies such as Integrated Sensing and Communications (ISAC), semantic communications, and Programmable Wireless Environments (PWE) built on Reconfigurable Intelligent Surfaces (RIS), 6G will move beyond passively adapting to the wireless environment and begin to actively shape it. This convergence, while enabling transformative applications, also fundamentally alters the threat model. New attack surfaces emerge at the intersection of sensing, communication, and AI, creating vulnerabilities that traditional higher-layer cryptography cannot sufficiently address.

This workshop seeks original contributions that advance the design of secure, private, and resilient 6G systems. We invite submissions that address security and privacy challenges from the physical layer up to the orchestration of PWEs. Topics of interest include, but are not limited to, novel theory, algorithms, system architectures, prototypes, testbeds, datasets, and measurements that tackle the unique cross-layer and AI-driven threats inherent in 6G. We welcome high-quality research that will pave the way for a trustworthy 6G infrastructure.

Topics of Interest

  • Security-by-design architectures for 6G and environment-aware networks
  • Zero-trust frameworks for programmable wireless environments (PWE)
  • AI-native security: online/continual learning, detection, response, and recovery
  • Privacy-preserving ISAC and joint sensing-communication learning pipelines
  • Secure reconfigurable intelligent surfaces (RIS) and metasurfaces (control, protocols, hardening)
  • Physical-layer security for mmWave, THz, and near-field communications
  • Semantics-aware security and defenses for semantic communications
  • Secure edge/cloud continuum: federated, split, and distributed AI for 6G
  • Adversarial ML in 6G (poisoning, evasion, backdoors) and robust training/inference
  • Differential privacy for sensing, localization, analytics, and traffic patterns
  • Secure network slicing, multi-tenant isolation, and intent-based orchestration
  • Privacy-preserving positioning, localization, mobility, and trajectory services
  • Cross-layer co-design across PHY/MAC/NET for security and privacy
  • Security and trust for digital twins, XR/metaverse, and cyber-physical systems
  • Quantum-enhanced security and key distribution; post-quantum cryptography for 6G
  • Blockchain/DLT for trust, identity, attestation, and auditing in 6G
  • Metrics, benchmarks, datasets, red-teaming, and open testbeds for secure 6G/PWE
